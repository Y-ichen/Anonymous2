Closed-source visual comparisons

These cases focus on the pattern emphasized in the written rebuttal: more precise localized editing, stronger instruction satisfaction under compound requests, and fewer collateral changes to non-edited regions such as pose, layout, walls, furniture, or background structure.

Key findings

Relative to strong closed-source editors, TC-FM+ is consistently more precise in the targeted edit region and causes fewer unintended changes to pose, layout, wall structure, or background context. The advantage is most visible on compound instructions, where competing models more often satisfy the edit by globally repainting the scene.
